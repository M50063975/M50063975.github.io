Abstract:
This article from Healthline explores the concept of teletherapy—online therapy sessions conducted through video, phone, or apps. It discusses the benefits and potential drawbacks of this format, especially in terms of accessibility, cost, and privacy. The article also offers advice on how to choose a teletherapist and what to expect during sessions. It’s written in a way that’s easy to understand for people who are new to mental health services and want to learn about virtual options. Overall, the article highlights how technology has opened new doors for receiving therapy without needing to visit a clinic or office.

Abstract:
This article from the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health discusses how digital technology is transforming mental health care, focusing on the rise of mental health apps. It emphasizes how mobile apps and other tech-based tools can offer support for anxiety, depression, and stress—especially for people who don’t have easy access to traditional care. The article also points out the importance of evidence-based practices and ethical concerns like data privacy. It’s written with a professional tone but still approachable for students and the general public. It supports the idea that apps can be a useful extension of therapy when used responsibly.
